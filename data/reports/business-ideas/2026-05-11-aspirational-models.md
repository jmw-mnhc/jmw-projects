## Aspirational case studies catalogued

### Adventure / Lifestyle Hospitality (6 case studies)
1. **Dunton Hot Springs** (Colorado) — 13-cabin all-inclusive at $2,400 ADR. The mini-Dunton play in your backyard = Castle Rock Springs (Middletown CA, $1.5M, 318 acres + thermal water). Direct cross-link in the dashboard.
2. **Lapa Rios Lodge** (Costa Rica) — eco-lodge + 1,000 acres + conservation moat. Maps to São Miguel do Gostoso, Costa Vicentina, Bahia coastal opportunities.
3. **The Ranch at Rock Creek** (Montana) — 6,600-acre Forbes 5-star. Lesson: if you can't assemble 6,600 acres, find the 100–500-acre version (Castle Rock Springs).
4. **Singita** (Africa) — concession-funded conservation luxury. Lesson: USFS rafting permits in CA are the same archetype.
5. **Eleven Experience** (multi-region) — heli/cat-ski/fish at $2k–$10k/day, all-inclusive whole-house buyout. Lesson: activity-first hospitality commands 2–3x the daily rate.
6. **OARS** (NorCal HQ) — family-founded rafting outfitter that scaled to largest commercial operator in western US on permits assembled before the '90s freeze. Lesson: permits ARE the moat. OARS won't sell — but 8–12 sub-OARS-scale outfitters with founder-aged owners will. → Cold-outreach campaign now in dashboard.

### Coffee / Roaster-Cafe (5 case studies)
7. **Sightglass Coffee** (SF) — roaster-cafe hybrid; wholesale (40–50% margin) cross-subsidizes retail (10–18% margin). Lesson: ROAST FIRST, RETAIL SECOND.
8. **Verve Coffee** (Santa Cruz) — surf-town brand scaled to multi-location international play; Japan licensing as 70%+ margin royalty. Lesson: place-of-origin authenticity is a moat money can't buy.
9. **Tartine Bakery** (SF) — bread + pastry margin (55–65%) anchors the kitchen; coffee is the supporting cast. Lesson: anchor the food side first.
10. **Caffe Trieste** (SF North Beach) — 70-year family institution. **OWN THE BUILDING.** The Giottas bought the Vallejo St building in 1956 — eliminating 25–35% of typical cafe P&L. Direct lesson for 1507 Prince + 1227 San Pablo Berkeley SBA 504 plays.
11. **Devoción** (Brooklyn) — vertically-integrated farm-to-cup; owns the Colombian farm + roastery + cafes. L Catterton growth equity in 2018. Lesson: vertical integration is a moat.
12. **Joe Coffee** (NYC) — family-warmth positioning at 25 stores, beat the third-wave coldness. Lesson: build for FAMILIES — Berkeley moms-with-strollers economy is enormous and underserved.

### Third-Place / Community Gathering (3 case studies)
13. **Diesel Cafe** (Somerville MA) — 25-year Davis Square living room. Pool tables + photo booth + game room + day-to-night drink program + queer-owned identity. Lesson: amenity stack costs little but creates differentiation.
14. **The Battery SF** (cautionary) — Birch-funded $50M+ private members club. Lesson: full-scale members club requires founder capital, but $30–80/mo cafe-coworking-membership at smaller scale works (Outsite proof).
15. **Sqirl** (LA) — closed brick-and-mortar in 2024 but jam + cookbook brand survived. Lesson: build a CPG product that travels — even when the cafe closes, the brand continues.

## The 10 profit levers (the layered-revenue framework)

| Lever | Margin | When to add | Revenue lift | Startup cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. **Wholesale roasting** | 40–50% | Day 1 — anchor the model | +$500k–$2M/yr | $30–100k roaster + $80k sales rep |
| 2. **DTC + subscription beans** | 45–55% | Within 6 months | $12k–$600k/yr recurring | $5k Shopify setup |
| 3. **Real food / kitchen** | 55–70% on food | Day 1 (counter); Year 1 (kitchen) | +150% revenue per customer | $50–200k full kitchen |
| 4. **Day-to-night flip (beer + wine)** | 60–70% | Year 1 if zoning supports | +30–80% top-line | $1–2k Type 41 license |
| 5. **Events + private rentals** | 60–80% | Year 1 | $30–200k/yr pure margin | Nominal |
| 6. **Membership / coworking layer** | 65%+ | Year 1 simple; Year 2 premium | $60k+ recurring | Stripe + member space |
| 7. **Branded merch + retail** | 40–70% | Year 1 (basics); Year 2 (CPG) | $1–15k/mo | $3–10k initial inventory |
| 8. **Real estate ownership** | Saves 25–35% of P&L | BEFORE signing any lease | 10–20pp net margin lift | 10% SBA 504 down |
| 9. **Education / classes** | 50–70% | Year 2+ | $15–60k/yr + brand value | Nominal |
| 10. **Catering + corporate** | 30–45% | Year 1 | $30–300k/yr | $500 boxes + 10 hrs/wk sales |

**The headline lesson:** Single-leg cafes lose money or barely break even. Stacking 4–6 of these levers is what separates Sightglass from a corner shop. The single biggest swing is **#8 (own the RE)** — eliminates 25–35% of typical cafe P&L. The 1507 Prince St lead is your textbook play.

## Off-market outreach campaigns now in dashboard

### Campaign A: NorCal Sierra rafting outfitters (5–7 year campaign)
**Target list (12):** Sierra Mac, All-Outdoors, Bigfoot, Zephyr, Beyond Limits, Tributary + 6 more to identify.
**Strategy:** Quarterly paper letters (this generation responds to letters, not email). Engage USFS-permit-transfer specialist attorney. Warm up two outfitter-network brokers (Greg Hipskind / Pat Conroy). Track founder ages + signal events.
**Why this matters:** USFS commercial rafting permits frozen since '90s = uncopyable moat. OARS-level deals will never publicly list. The only way in is succession-outreach.

### Campaign B: Bay Area third-place cafe institutions (3–5 year campaign)
**Target list (6):** Caffe Trieste, Cafe Strada (Berkeley), Caffe Greco (SF), Mama's Royal (Oakland), Saul's (Berkeley), Brennan's (Berkeley).
**Strategy:** Confirm founder age + RE ownership for each. Send paper letters emphasizing brand preservation + family-operator continuity + transition flexibility. Engage Compass Commercial East Bay (already on outreach list for 1507 Prince) for off-market hospitality + RE-bundled deals.
**Why this matters:** The Caffe Trieste model (own-the-building + day-to-night flip) is the highest-margin third-place archetype. Public listings = zero.

### Campaign C: SF Bay sailing schools (2–4 year campaign, OCSC priority)
**Target list (4):** OCSC (Berkeley Marina — priority match), Modern Sailing (Sausalito), Tradewinds (Pt Richmond), Spinnaker (Redwood City).
**Strategy:** OCSC Sandberg founded 1979, age 70+, Berkeley match = the dream target. Get ASA-certified BEFORE outreach to enter as a peer, not a buyer. Engage Berkeley Yacht Club + St Francis Yacht Club for warm intros. ASA national org sometimes facilitates school transitions.
**Why this matters:** Harbor slip rights + ASA accreditation chain + recurring charter-club membership = three textbook moats. Founder generation aging into succession; almost nothing trades publicly.
