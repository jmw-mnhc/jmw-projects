## Deeper insights from the sweep

### Wellness sweep — the single biggest signal
Bay Area cold plunge / sauna bathhouses are STRUCTURALLY DRY in the secondary market. Reboot Float, Kume, Fjord, Perspire are all owner-operated, recently launched, in capex-build phase. **This is a greenfield opportunity, not an acquisition.** Pair with the 1507 Prince St SBA 504 owner-user thesis = bathhouse as anchor tenant in your own building. Othership (Toronto/NYC) is the proven template. The /aspirational-models workspace has a full case study + the /playbook has the build-team outreach template.

### Brewery sweep — Snowshoe is the call
Snowshoe Brewery + Restaurant in Arnold CA is the single best near-term action item from this entire deeper/wider sweep:

- RE-included = SBA 7(a) + 504 stack

### Vineyard sweep — Sierra Foothills is the in-box CA play
Sonoma + Napa core priced out at $4.81M average list. Sierra Foothills (El Dorado, Placer, Amador) is where the buy box clears. Kehret ($1.45M) and Lone Buffalo ($1.97M) are the two live in-box options — both have winery + tasting room + named-winemaker brand. Add a 2-suite barn-conversion B&B for ~$400k = Tablas Creek model at quarter the price.

### Multi-unit chain sweep — closed-loop = outreach campaign
Climbing chain rollups don't list publicly (El Cap PE / Movement / Touchstone closed-loop). The 4-Location SoCal Pilates Group is the cleanest publicly-listed multi-unit chain match. For climbing specifically, the path is direct outreach to PE shops for non-strategic single-unit divestitures (now an active campaign).

### "I'm not Jellystone" positioning
The Martha's Vineyard Family Campground sale to Jellystone Parks (Apr 2026) is a cautionary case study — sellers who don't want their multi-generational legacy corporatized are explicitly looking for family-operator buyers. The cold-outreach templates in /playbook lean into this: "I'm not Jellystone" is the counter-narrative that wins on emotion, not just price.
